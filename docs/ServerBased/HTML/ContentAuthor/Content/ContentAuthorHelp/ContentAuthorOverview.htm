Overview of how Content Author works

The basic process for updating content in Content Author is simple:

Select the customer communication that you want to update

You begin in Content Launcher by selecting from a list of available communications or by creating a new communication from a list of templates. If you have questions about the templates that are available to you, talk to the person or people who designs communications in your Exstream environment.

From the Content Author Content Launcher, do one of the following:

Open an existing communication

  1. To see the communications that are available to you, click one of the following buttons:

    • Existing—Select from a list of existing communications that designers or Content Author users have created.

    • Recent—Select from the list of the most recent communications that you have opened in Content Author.

    • Favorites—Select from the list of communications that you have marked as favorites. (To make a communication a favorite, simply point to the name of a communication in the lists on the Communication or Recent tabs, and then click .)

  2. Click Open.

    The communication opens in Communications view.

Create a new communication

  1. Click New.

  2. Select a template from the list. If you are not sure which template to select, you can select a template and then click Preview to see print or HTML previews of each.

  3. Click Next.

  4. Enter a name and an optional description for your communication, then click Create.

    The communication opens in Communications view.

    Tip: Be as clear as possible when creating titles and descriptions for your communications, to make it easy to differentiate between the communications that are available in Content Launcher.

Select a frame within the customer communication to create, edit, or remove content

In Communications view you can see the communication in its entirety, which allows you to understand how the editable content fits together with other design elements to form the overall customer communication. Depending on the design of your communication, you can optionally switch between the PDF and HTML preview types.

  1. If multiple preview types are available, select a preview type from the toolbar:

    • To see the PDF version of the communication, click .

    • To see the HTML version of the communication, click .

    Note: If your communication will be sent to customers as only a PDF or as only HTML, you will not have the option to switch between the two preview types.

  2. Select a view:

    • To see larger pages, click .

    • To see thumbnail pages, click .

    • To see areas where you can modify content, click . (You can also right-click the Applied Content card and then click Show frames.)

      When this option is selected, editable content frames are highlighted (light blue for clauses, and light purple for text and graphic messages).

  3. Click an available (highlighted) frame where you want to add or modify content.

    Note that some frames might contain clauses that will be made available for editing later in Exstream Empower Editor. If you have questions about content that will be edited in Empower Editor, you should talk to the person who designed the communication.

  4. In the Applied Content section, on the card for the selected frame, do one of the following:

    Task Action
    Create new content for a graphic message or text message

    1. Click , or right-click the card for the frame and then click Add new content.

    2. In the Content Composer dialog box, click the New content tab.

    3. Add a name and description for the content, to help differentiate it from other content.

    4. Click Create.

      The content opens in Authoring view to be edited.

    Create new content for a clause
    1. Do one of the following:
      • Click and then click Add clause.
      • Right-click the card for the frame and then click Add new content > Add clause.

    2. In the Content Composer dialog box, click the New content tab.

    3. Add a name and description for the content, to help differentiate it from other content.

    4. Click Create.

      The content opens in Authoring view to be edited.

    Use a section to hold clauses or other sections

    You can think of sections as containers that let you organize not only clauses, but also other sections. And organization is easy: In Communications view or Authoring view, you can drag and drop sections and clauses to organize the hierarchy of content within a parent section.

    Note that this procedure assumes that you are starting from Communications view, and that you are creating a brand new section.

    To use a section to hold clauses or other sections:

    1. Do one of the following:
      • Click and then click Add section.
      • Right-click the card for the frame and then click Add new content > Add section.

    2. In the Content Composer dialog box, click the New content tab.

    3. Add a title and description for the section, to help differentiate it from other sections.

    4. Click Create.

      The section appears on the card for the frame.

    5. To add sections or clauses to the section, do one of the following:

      • Click the icon next to the name of the section to expand the content tree for the section. With the content tree expanded, you can drag and drop existing clauses or sections to add them to the section. If the section contains no clauses, you will see (Empty) when you expand the content tree. When you have multiple clauses or sections in place, you can change the order by dragging and dropping them on the card for the frame.

      • Double-click the name of the section to open it in Authoring view. In Authoring view, you can click to add sections or clauses as needed. When you have multiple clauses or sections in place, you can change the order by dragging and dropping them on the Section map card.

    6. Click on the right side of the clause or section to save the changes and return to the parent section. If you click , you will be prompted about any changes that you made during the session, and then you return to the parent section.

    Tip: As you work with nested sections, keep in mind that opening sections and clauses in Authoring view reserves each one until you return to Communications view. Sections or clauses that are reserved are not available for other users to work on.
    Add existing content to the frame

    1. Click , or right-click the card for the frame and then click Add new content.

    2. In the Content Composer dialog box, click the Existing content tab.

    3. From the list, select the existing content that you want to add.

    4. Click Insert.

    5. Optionally, you can edit the content that you just added to the frame by selecting it from the list and then clicking .

      The content opens in Authoring view to be edited.

      Tip: You can also right-click the name of the content and then click Edit content to open the content directly in Authoring view.
    Edit existing content in the frame

    1. From the list of content that is available for the frame, select the content that you want to edit.

    2. Double-click the content in the frame, or clickon the card for the frame.

      The content opens in Authoring view to be edited.

      Tip: You can also right-click the name of the content and then click Edit content to open the content directly in Authoring view.
    Remove existing content from the frame

    1. From the list of content that is available for the frame, select the content that you want to remove.

    2. Click (or right-click the name of the content and then click Remove content), and then confirm your choice in the dialog box that opens.

      The content is removed from the frame but remains available for use in the communication.
    See where the content is being used in other communications

    1. From the list of content that is available for the frame, select a piece of content.

      The other communications that use the selected content appear in the Affected Communications list below the card for the frame.

    2. To open a communication to see how the selected content is being used, double-click the name of the communication.

      The communication opens in Communications view.

Tip: In Communications view, you can see metadata about a piece of content by clicking on the toolbar. The metadata opens in the Communication details dialog box. A subset of metadata also appears on the Details card for the content. You can also open the Communication details dialog box by clicking Advanced on the Details card.

Make changes to the content

You have different options for the types of content that you can add to a frame, depending on whether you are working with a graphic message, a text message, a section, or a clause. Naturally, graphic messages can contain a mixture of text and graphics, and users can place content anywhere within the content frame. Text messages and clauses can contain the same elements that graphic messages can contain (text, images, and so on); the difference is that all of the elements in a text message or clause are placed inline like text.

Content Author provides familiar tools for you to use to add content—tools that you might find in any word processing application. The biggest difference between Content Author and a standard word processor is that Content Author uses variables and rules to control how content is targeted to individual customers. The overall communication is divided between areas where you can make changes, and areas that remain static.

Save your changes

After you have made any needed changes to your content, you simply click on the right side of the content to save the changes and return either to Communications view or to the parent section of the content. If you click , any changes that you made during the session are discarded and you return either to Communications view or to the parent section of the content.

Review your updated messages within the larger customer communication

Back in Communications view, the updates that you made to the content appear in the larger communication. This step in the process allows you to make sure that the changes you made are consistent with the communication as a whole. You can return to Content Launcher by clicking > Return to Content Launcher.

Tip: Refresh your browser window before making changes to a communication to ensure that you are seeing the latest content in Communications view. It is particularly important to refresh your browser if multiple users might be working in a communication at the same time.

Use workflow to send your content or communication through the approval process

Only approved content and communications can be used in customer output. After you have finished making changes, you must use the Content Author workflow tools to send messages, clauses, sections, and communications for approval.

Depending on the design, after your content and communications are approved, the communication might be populated with customer data and sent to customers without further changes or additions, or it might be dynamically added to an even more sophisticated communication that includes other customer-specific elements. If you have questions about the workflow of your communication after your changes in Content Author are complete, you should talk to the person who designed the communication.
