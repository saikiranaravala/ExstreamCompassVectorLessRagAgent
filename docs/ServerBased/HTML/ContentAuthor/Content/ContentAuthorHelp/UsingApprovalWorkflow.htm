Using approval workflows

In Content Author, the term "approval workflow" refers to the progression of content or of a communication from initial version to final approval. You can use a workflow to approve the individual pieces of content that make up communications, or to approve entire communications (including content). Using an approval workflow helps to ensure that new and updated content is properly reviewed for accuracy and conforms to company standards.

It is important to understand that the approval workflow for content is separate from the approval workflow for communications. The approval workflow for communications comes into play only when you add, reorder, or remove content. Keep in mind that a customer communication might also include content that is managed by other business units, outside of the Content Author environment. If you have questions about the content in a customer communication, you should talk to the person who designed the communication.

Note: Your ability to move content or communications through an approval workflow is determined by access privileges within your organization’s Exstream environment. If you have questions about your access privileges, speak to your system administrator.

Use the following information to guide your content and communications through the Content Author approval workflow:

Overview of the approval process

At a high level, the approval process for content and communications is as follows:

  1. A content creator makes changes or updates to content or to a communication.
  2. The content creator finishes editing the content or communication and then submits it for approval.
  3. An approver either accepts or rejects the updates. If the approver accepts the updates, the content or communication is ready to be used in customer output. If the approver rejects the updates, then the content creator must return the content to draft status and guide it through the approval process again.

The example above illustrates the life cycle of content that you control in Content Author; customer communications might include other content that is managed by other business units outside of the Content Author environment. If you have questions about the content in a customer communication, you should talk to the person who designed the communication.

Approval considerations for sections and clauses

As you work with sections and clauses in your communication, it is also important to understand the relationship between a clause and its parent section. When you add a clause to a section, it is the clause itself—and not a particular version of the clause—that is associated with the section.

For example, suppose you create a section and then add two clauses to it. After you have approved both clauses, you then approve the parent section. If you later realize that you need to change the content in one of the clauses, you do not need to create a draft version of the section. Instead, you can simply create a draft version of the clause and then guide that clause through approval workflow in Content Author. In that scenario, the workflow state of the section does not change. When the new version of the clause is approved, it will appear automatically in any output that uses the section.

If you need to make structural changes to an approved section, such as adding, removing, or reordering clauses within the section, then you must create a draft version of the section and then guide it through the approval process.

Note: All clauses within a section must have at least one previously approved version before the section can be approved. If you try to approve a section that contains a clause that has never been approved, Content Author issues an error message and the section does not move into an approved state.

Reserve the communication for editing

Because other users in your organization might need to work on a communication at the same time that you do, you should always make sure that you are the only one working on a communication before you make changes. You automatically reserve communications by making structural changes to them (such as adding, moving, or deleting content). If you try to make changes to a communication that someone else has reserved, you will receive a message and will be unable to make changes. To make sure that no one else has a communication reserved, simply refresh your browser in Communications view.

To reserve a communication for editing, or to see who has a communication reserved:

  1. With a communication open in Communications view, refresh your browser session. Refreshing your browser ensures not only that you know whether the communication is currently reserved by another user, but also that you are looking at the most up-to-date content in the communication. It is particularly important to refresh your browser if multiple users are working in a communication at the same time.

  2. On the toolbar, check the status indicator for the communication:

    • If you see , then the communication is currently reserved. You can point to the lock icon to see who currently has the communication reserved.
    • If you see , then the communication is available for editing. You can either make a structural change (such as adding, moving, or deleting content) to automatically reserve the communication, or you can click the slider to toggle between reserving and releasing the communication.
Use visual indicators to quickly determine workflow states

You can quickly determine the current workflow status of content or of a communication by looking for the workflow symbols on each. The following table explains the meaning of each workflow symbol:

Indicator Workflow state What it means
Draft The content or communication is available for editing.
Submitted for approval The content or communication has been submitted for approval and cannot be edited. To make changes to content or to a communication that has been submitted for approval, you must return the content or communication to a draft state.
Approved The content or communication is approved and is ready to be used in customer output. To make changes to content or to a communication that has been approved, you must create a new draft.
Rejected The content or communication has been rejected and cannot be edited. To make changes to content or to a communication that has been rejected, you must return the content or communication to a draft state.

When in Communications view, you can find the workflow state for the communication itself by looking for the workflow symbol next to the name of the communication on the Selected Communication card. The workflow state for content appears next to the name of each piece of content in the Applied Content section. When you are editing content, you can see its current workflow state on the bottom button to the right of the canvas in Authoring view.

Use workflow to move content through the approval process

As you move content through the approval process, keep in mind that only draft content that is not currently being edited by another user can be edited. After content has been either submitted for approval or rejected, you must return the content to a draft state in order to add to or update it. Similarly, if content has already been approved, you must create a new draft of the content in order to add to or update it.

If a red line appears just below the content canvas in Authoring view and the editing tools are not available, it is either because the content is no longer in a draft state or another user is currently editing it.

To check the current workflow state of the content, simply look for the workflow symbol on the bottom button to the right of the content canvas.

Note: Your ability to move content or communications through an approval workflow is determined by access privileges within your organization’s Exstream environment. If you have questions about your access privileges, speak to your system administrator.

Use workflow to move a communication through the approval process

When you work with communications, it is important to remember that you can submit a communication for approval, or you can reject a submitted communication without affecting the workflow state of the content that it contains. If you try to approve a communication that contains unapproved content (or content that does not have a previously approved version), you will receive a message indicating that you must first approve all of the content within the communication before you can approve the communication itself. If you approve a communication that contains draft content with a previously approved version, the approved communication will use the previously approved content, not the draft content.

To check the current workflow state of a communication, simply look for the workflow symbol next to the name of the communication on the Selected Communication card in Communications view.

As with content, after a communication has been either submitted for approval or rejected, you must return it to a draft state in order to add to or update it. Similarly, if a communication has already been approved, you must create a new draft of the communication in order to add to or update it. That draft is considered a completely separate version of the communication and must be approved separately in order to be used for customer output.

Note: Your ability to move content or communications through an approval workflow is determined by access privileges within your organization’s Exstream environment. If you have questions about your access privileges, speak to your system administrator.
