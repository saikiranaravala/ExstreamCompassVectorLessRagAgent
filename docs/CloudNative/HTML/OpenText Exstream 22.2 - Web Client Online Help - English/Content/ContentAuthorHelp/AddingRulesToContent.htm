Adding rules to content and communications

Rules allow you to set content or communications to be included or excluded based on customer data. For example, suppose that you want to include content in a communication only if the customer owns a particular type of vehicle. To accomplish this, you can apply a rule to the content that looks for a particular type of vehicle. When the communication is produced as output, if the customer data indicates that the customer owns the specified vehicle type, then the content is included in the communication; otherwise, the content is excluded. Similarly, you can include or exclude entire communications for particular customers by applying rules in Communications view.

Content Author lets you apply two types of rules to content and communications:

  • Library rules—These rules are set up by the person who designs the communication.

  • Custom rules—You build these rules from scratch based on conditions that you set.

Note: You might not have access to custom rules if there are no variables in your communication, or if your user role does not allow you to create custom rules. If you have questions about the variables that are available in a communication, you should talk to the person who designed it. If you have questions about your user role, you should talk to your system administrator.

Adding rules to messages, sections, or clauses

  1. In Communications view with content selected on the Applied Content card, do one of the following:

    • Click on the toolbar.

    • Right-click the name of the message, section, or clause and then click Edit rule.

    Tip: You can also add rules to messages or clauses in Authoring view by clicking on the toolbar.

  2. In the Rule Composer dialog, do one of the following:

    Task Action
    Add a library rule

    1. Click the Library rule tab.

    2. Select a rule from the list, then click Insert.
    Build a new rule

    1. Click the Custom rule tab.

    2. To create a condition for your rule, select whether to include or exclude content based on the rule.

    3. Populate the fields as needed with the variables, operators, and values that will define the condition for the rule.

    4. Optionally, click to add another condition to your rule, or click to remove a condition.

    5. Click Insert.

After you have applied the rule to your content, the content is included or excluded in customer communications based on whether or not the customer data meets the conditions of the rule. Keep in mind that the content will not appear in Communications view if the sample data meets the conditions of an exclusion rule. However, you can use the Content Author data simulation tools to see how the content behaves in different data scenarios.

Removing rules

  1. Do one of the following:

    • In Communications view, on the Selected Communication card, click the name of the communication.

    • In Communications view, on the Applied Content card, select the name of a message, clause, or section.

    • Open a message or clause in Authoring view.

  2. On the toolbar, click .

    3. Tip: In Communications view, you can also right-click the name of a message, clause, or section, and then click Edit rule.

  3. In the Rule Composer dialog, click Remove, and then click Clear on the confirmation dialog.
