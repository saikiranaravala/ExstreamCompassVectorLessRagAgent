Using approval workflows

In Content Author, the term "approval workflow" refers to the progression of content or of a communication from initial version to final approval. You can use a workflow to approve the individual pieces of content that make up communications, or to approve entire communications (including content). Using an approval workflow helps to ensure that new and updated content is properly reviewed for accuracy and conforms to company standards.

Types of approval workflows

Your Exstream administrator configures Communications DesignerContent Author to use one of the following types of approval workflows in each domain:

  • Standard workflow—requires you to submit your changes for approval.

  • Simple workflow—allows you to skip the "submit for approval" step and approve your own changes.

  • No workflow—allows you to skip the approval workflow or use a different approval workflow that your organization defines.

Approval workflows for content and communications

In the standard and simple workflows, the approval workflow for content is separate from the approval workflow for communications. The approval workflow for communications comes into play only when you add, reorder, or remove content. Keep in mind that a customer communication might also include content that is managed by other business units, outside of the Content Author environment. If you have questions about the content in a customer communication, you should talk to the person who designed the communication.

Note: Your ability to move content or communications through an approval workflow is determined by access privileges within your organization’s Exstream environment. If you have questions about your access privileges, speak to your system administrator.

Approval workflow steps

You follow these steps to apply the standard or simple workflow to the content and communications that you manage in Content Author:

  1. Start the workflow by changing a piece of content or a communication.

    Note: A content creator might only be able to change some pieces of content in a communication. The other pieces might be managed by business units outside of Content Author.

  2. Save your changes to the content or communication.

  3. If you use the standard workflow, then submit your changes for approval.

    • If the changes are rejected, then return content or communication to draft status, update the returned draft, and resubmit the changes for approval.

    • If the changes are accepted, then the content or communication is ready for customer output.

  4. If you use the simple workflow, then approve your changes. The content or communication is ready for customer output.
Approval considerations for sections and clauses

Adding a clause to a parent section associates the clause itself with the section—not a particular version of the clause. As result, you can approve a parent section if each clause in the section has at least one approved version. The approved version of each clause can be the latest version or any older version.

For example, suppose you create a section and then add two clauses to it. After you have approved both clauses, you then approve the parent section. If you later realize that you need to change the content in one of the clauses, you do not need to create a draft version of the section. Instead, you can simply create a draft version of the clause and then guide that clause through approval workflow in Content Author. In that scenario, the workflow state of the section does not change. When the new version of the clause is approved, it will appear automatically in any output that uses the section.

If you need to make structural changes to an approved section, such as adding, removing, or reordering clauses within the section, then you must create a draft version of the section and then guide it through the approval process.

Note: All clauses within a section must have at least one previously approved version before the section can be approved. If you try to approve a section that contains a clause that has never been approved, Content Author issues an error message and the section does not move into an approved state.

Reserving a communication for editing

Because other users in your organization might need to work on a communication at the same time that you do, you should always make sure that you are the only one working on a communication before you make changes. You automatically reserve communications by making structural changes to them (such as adding, moving, or deleting content). If you try to make changes to a communication that someone else has reserved, you will receive a message and will be unable to make changes. To make sure that no one else has a communication reserved, simply refresh your browser in Communications view.

To reserve a communication for editing, or to see who has a communication reserved:

  1. With a communication open in Communications view, refresh your browser session. Refreshing your browser ensures not only that you know whether the communication is currently reserved by another user, but also that you are looking at the most up-to-date content in the communication. It is particularly important to refresh your browser if multiple users are working in a communication at the same time.

  2. On the toolbar, check the status indicator for the communication:

    • If you see , then the communication is currently reserved. You can point to the lock icon to see who currently has the communication reserved.
    • If you see , then the communication is available for editing. You can either make a structural change (such as adding, moving, or deleting content) to automatically reserve the communication, or you can click the slider to toggle between reserving and releasing the communication.

Moving content through the approval workflow

As you move content through the approval workflow, keep in mind that only draft content that is not currently being edited by another user can be edited. In the standard workflow, after content has been either submitted for approval or rejected, you must return the content to a draft state in order to add to or update it. In the standard or simple workflow, if content has already been approved, you must create a new draft of the content in order to add to or update it.

If a red line appears just below the content canvas in Authoring view and the editing tools are not available, it is either because the content is no longer in a draft state or another user is currently editing it.

To check the current workflow state of the content, simply look for the workflow symbol on the Change workflow state button at the bottom right of the content canvas.

Note: Your ability to move content or communications through an approval workflow is determined by access privileges within your organization’s Exstream environment. If you have questions about your access privileges, speak to your system administrator.

Moving a communication through the approval workflow

When you work with communications in the standard workflow, you can submit a communication for approval, or you can reject a submitted communication without affecting the workflow state of the content that it contains. However, in the standard or simple workflow, if you try to approve a communication that contains unapproved content (or content that does not have a previously approved version), you will receive a message indicating that you must first approve all of the content within the communication before you can approve the communication itself. If you approve a communication that contains draft content with a previously approved version, the approved communication will use the previously approved content, not the draft content.

To check the current workflow state of a communication, simply look for the workflow symbol next to the name of the communication on the Selected Communication card in Communications view.

In the standard workflow, as with content, after a communication has been either submitted for approval or rejected, you must return it to a draft state in order to add to or update it. In the standard or simple workflow, if a communication has already been approved, you must create a new draft of the communication in order to add to or update it. That draft is considered a completely separate version of the communication and must be approved separately in order to be used for customer output.

Note: Your ability to move content or communications through an approval workflow is determined by access privileges within your organization’s Exstream environment. If you have questions about your access privileges, speak to your system administrator.
